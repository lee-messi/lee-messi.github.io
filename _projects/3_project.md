In this project, we build on top of the foundation laid out by [my previous work on homogeneity bias in LLMs](https://openreview.net/forum?id=izC60DLg29). In this project, we expand beyond this work by 1) employing text completion prompts to examine jobs that LLMs associate with various gender and racial/ethnic groups, and by 2) proposing a novel measure of homogeneity in LLM-generated text - entropy. This measure quantifies homogeneity bias by assessing the uncertainty of LLM-generated text (i.e., jobs, industry) across groups. Our analysis reveals two key findings: 1) Jobs associated with females are more homogeneous than those associated with males; and 2) Jobs associated with Asian Americans are more homogeneous than those associated with other racial\slash ethnic groups. This work not only provides clear evidence of homogeneity bias in LLMs but also establishes a critical benchmark for future research aimed at evaluating and mitigating this form of bias.
